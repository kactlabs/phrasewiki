## "Cry over spilled milk" 🥛

**Meaning:** The phrase means to waste time and emotional energy on something that can't be changed or fixed, rather than moving forward and focusing on what's possible.

---

### Origin
The phrase is believed to have originated in the 15th century, when it was used as a proverb to caution against excessive regret over past mistakes. It is thought to have come from the idea that crying over spilled milk would be pointless, as it wouldn't change the outcome of an accident.

---

### Real-Time Example

Imagine Sarah accidentally drops her expensive smartphone on the floor during rush hour. She's devastated and tries to pick it up, but it's too late - it's out of commission. As she stands there, staring at the broken screen, a friend walks by and says, "Cry over spilled milk! You can just get another one." Sarah takes a deep breath, puts away her tears, and heads to the nearest phone repair shop.

---

### Other Everyday Contexts

| Situation | Usage |
|---|---|
| Missing a job opportunity due to poor timing | *"[You're wasting your time feeling sorry for not getting that job; focus on applying to others!]"* |
| Feeling sad about a past relationship that's over | *"[It's been months since you two broke up, why dwell on what's gone? Focus on meeting new people and finding someone else!"]* |
| Making a mistake at work and feeling guilty | *"[You can't undo the damage, but you can learn from it and do better next time; don't let it hold you back!]"* |

---

**In short:** Don't waste your time and energy dwelling on what's already happened - focus on what's ahead.
