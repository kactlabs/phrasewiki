## "Spill the beans" 🌽️
**Meaning:** To reveal a secret or surprise prematurely, often causing others to feel disappointed or betrayed.

---

### Origin
The origin of "spill the beans" is unclear, but it's believed to have originated in the United States in the late 19th century. One possible explanation is that it came from the tradition of opening beans at a dinner party before the main course was served, thereby spoiling the surprise for the guests.

---

### Real-Time Example

Imagine you're planning a surprise birthday party for your friend, and you've been keeping it a secret until the big reveal. Just as you're about to shout "Surprise!" and the guest of honor walks into the room, their partner accidentally reveals the news on social media. In this situation, someone might say:

> "Oh no! You spilled the beans!"

---

### Other Everyday Contexts

| Situation | Usage |
|---|---|
| If you're planning a surprise gift for someone and they accidentally see it before the big reveal: *"[You] really spilled the beans!"* |
| When someone reveals confidential information that was meant to be kept secret: *"[They] spilled the beans on our top-secret project!"* |
| In a game of charades or Pictionary, if someone gives away the answer too early: *"[They] really spilled the beans with that guess!"* |

---

**In short:** Revealing a surprise or secret prematurely, often causing others to feel disappointed or betrayed.
