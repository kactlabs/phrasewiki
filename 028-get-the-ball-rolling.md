## "Get the ball rolling" 🏀

**Meaning:** To initiate or start a process, conversation, or activity that will lead to progress or momentum.

---

### Origin
The phrase is believed to have originated in the mid-19th century, when balls were used as symbols of power and influence. In this context, "getting the ball rolling" meant gaining control over a situation or project by securing its initial funding or support.

---

### Real-Time Example

Imagine you're part of a team planning a group project for work. You've been assigned to lead the initiative, but everyone is waiting for someone else to take charge. To get the momentum going, you say: 
>
> "Alright guys, let's get the ball rolling! Who's available to meet at 2 pm tomorrow to finalize our ideas?"
>
>
> The team members start chattering excitedly, sharing their thoughts and ideas, and soon the project starts gaining traction.

---

### Other Everyday Contexts

| Situation | Usage |
|---|---|
| Planning a party | "Let's get the ball rolling on invitations and decorations!" |
| Starting a new business venture | "If we can secure funding, we'll be able to get the ball rolling on our startup." |
| Hosting a meeting | "Can someone please get the ball rolling by summarizing the agenda?" |

---

**In short:** Get the ball rolling is about starting something that will lead to progress and momentum.
