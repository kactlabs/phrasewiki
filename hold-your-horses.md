## "Hold Your Horses" 🐴

**Meaning:** Slow down, be patient, don't act too hastily. It's a way of telling
someone to pause and wait before rushing into something.

---

### Origin
The phrase dates back to the era of horse-drawn carriages and cavalry, where riders
literally had to *hold* (restrain) their horses to prevent them from bolting or
moving too soon.

---

### Real-Time Example

Imagine you're at a **Kactii GenAI Bootcamp** session, and a student sees a viral
LinkedIn post claiming *"This new open-source model beats GPT-4 on every benchmark!"*
and immediately wants to scrap the entire llama.cpp setup to switch frameworks overnight.

You'd say:

> **"Hold your horses!** Let's actually read the benchmark methodology, check the VRAM
> requirements, and see if it even runs on our RTX 3090 before we tear down everything
> we've built."

The student is rushing based on hype. The phrase pumps the brakes — verify first, act second.

---

### Other Everyday Contexts

| Situation | Usage |
|---|---|
| A friend books flights before confirming vacation days | *"Hold your horses — did you check if you have time off approved?"* |
| A developer pushes code to production without testing | *"Hold your horses — has this gone through QA?"* |
| Someone jumps to conclusions in an argument | *"Hold your horses — let me finish explaining."* |

---

**In short:** It's the verbal equivalent of hitting the brakes — a calm, firm *"not so fast."*
